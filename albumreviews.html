Album Reviews - Selected Art

Album Reviews

Here are albums I suggest for listening.

Artist - Album - URL:

Alestorm, Captain Morgans Revenge - board the plank ye' scurrrvy scallywags, because Alestorm is an awesome pirate metal band, plenty of sea shantys combined with a good metal mood, and ridiculous lyrics. Alestorm is an experience you will never forget.

Amity In Fame, Dinner For One - quite a nice combination of acoustic guitar mixed with a class hard rock feel. strong carrying vocals and interesting guitar riffs, overall this album is definitely an oringinal one.

Apparatjik, We Are Here - it's hard to explain what this album is, nothing short of genius. Guy Barryman ( Coldplay ) teamed up with Jonas of Mew brings you an techno driven pop album that will draw you in for many listens. We Are Here is creepy with an odd combination of sounds. tracks like Datascroller will make you tap your foot while Arrow And Bow will give you goosebumps.

Band Of Horses - Infinite Arms

Carbon Leaf, Echo Echo - Last year one of my good friends introduced me to Carbon Leaf, and ever since they have been one of my relax bands. Echo Echo is an excellent album, it is extremely mellow and catchy. favorite tracks? I couldn't tell you. they all rock.

Circa Survive, Blue Sky Noise

Coheed and Cambria, The Second Stage Turbine Blade - this is hands down the greatest concept album ever created in the history of mankind, far surpassing anything The Beatles or Pink Floyd did. words to descibe the feeling i get while listening to this are almost impossible to find. gut wrenching lyrics, detailed background guitar, ridiculous bass lines and an insane drummer, all coupled into an album. the concept, is hard to fathom. a giant science fiction fantasy about Claudio Kilgannon, taking place in an alternative universe, that spans across 5 albums, there is no story better drawn than The Amory Wars - the underlying story behind Coheed and Cambria's music. The Second Stage Turbine Blade started it all for me, take the time to listen to it, i wouldn't be surprised if you didn't like it, but stick with it and you'll find yourself blow away.

The Dada Weatherman - Mind Of Blue

Davenport Cabinet, Nostalgia In Stereo - Travis Stever, Lead Guitarist for the previously noted Coheed and Cambria takes a vastly different approach than his typical ridiculous shredding and screeching solos. In fact, this album is quite relaxing. It's a folk album but has a definite undertone of classic rock roots. Travis has a strange voice that ends up fitting in perfectly. I listen to this album quite a bit.

Death Cab For Cutie - Plans

Diablo Swing Orchestra - The Butchers Ballroom

Flat People, Self Titled - yet another self titled album that is awesome. Flat People is a groovy chill album incorperating alot of cool sounds. It sort of reminds me of Pink Floyd yet has distinctly non floydish tendencies. Check it out, and give it a listen. Awesome builds and smooth vocals pull it together nicely.

Flobots, Fight With Tools - definitely one of the most influential albums i've listened to. being of sound mind and moral stature, i cannot help but question our global condition after listening to Fight With Tools. this album will certainly make you second guess America's bureaucracy. it is a call to arms to every listener to stand up and fight. give it a listen, you might find yourself on a path that will open your mind.

Good Old War, Only Way To Be Alone -

Green Day, American Idiot - in fifth grade, i purchased my first CD, American Idiot. since then i have listened to it numerous times and have the entire album memorized. Green Day is a classic punk band of the 90's, yet this album has a very different feel than Dookie or Warning. an excellent concept album, it encapsulates a variety of styles and instruments. i consider this by far one of the most influential albums of the 2000s, and the best album Green Day has released. give it a go, and you will soon be singing along.

Hot Hot Heat - Elevator

Houdini Roadshow - Down in the City

Jonathan Coulton, Smoking Monkey - while he isn't much of the album type, Jonathan Coulton continually comes again and again with single songs that make the top nerd charts. he has an excellent writing style and a relaxed voice. funny and interesting lyrics make sure that you will be singing them in your head. I would suggest you not check this album, but more of his singles

John Mayer Trio - Try!

The Liquid Kitchen - 2 Days A Night

major Major - Major Major

Mew, Frengers -  Frengers is really an interesting peice of art. Jonas has a smooth voice that blends in with the vocals, the music is extremely relaxed yet the drums are upbeat and sometimes even a bit harsh. the music just flows in and out in this album. Frengers is a portmanteu of friends and strangers, and that is what the album feels like, they are strangers but you get to know them throughout the album. awesome builds and intense lyrics, mew is one of the great indie bands out there.

Mew - And The Glass Handed Kites

My Chemical Romance - Three Cheers For Sweet Revenge

NanowaR - Other Bands Play, Nanowar Gay!

Nightwish - Wishmaster

Phantom Planet,  Raise The Dead - easily Phantom Planet's finest record, this work of art was enthralling. like a good book, i simply could not put it down. every song on this album is incredible. it carries an extremely distinct sound, yet all the songs use different elements to create a diverse album. don't miss out on it, because you will come back to Raise The Dead again and again.

pornophonique - 8-bit lagerfeuer

The Proclaimers - Sunshine on Leith - What's more cheesy than these scottish brothers singing songs to make girls swoon? I have no idea - but this album is one of my favorites.

Shabutie - Plan to Take Over the World

Silversun Pickups - Swoon

Sum 41 - All Killer No Filler

Talco - Combat Circus

Typical Cats - Typical Cats - The Typical Cats rap collective is fantastic. Qwel, Qwazaar, Denizen Kane and DJ Natural tear it up in this sick rap album. Fantastic samples, even better raps, whitty lyrics and awesome scratching make this group some of the best underground rap out of Chicago.

The Wonder Years - Suburbia, You've Taken Everything And Now I'm Nothing - If you like Philadelphia written, suburban bordom driven, pop punk drivel of a frustrated guy in his mid 20's, this album is for you. 9/10

Zac Brown Band - Pass The Jar - A really great live album from Zac Brown, a contemporary southern/country singer. He has a really strong band, and this album features a wide array of guest singers which makes it really diverse. A great example of country done right. A good mix of oldies and originals.
